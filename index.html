How It Works

This "Eco-F1 Builder" allows you to select various components for a conceptual F1 car, each with its own impact on:

  • Performance: How fast the car can go.
  • Cost: The estimated financial outlay for the component.
  • Eco-Score: A simplified metric representing the component's environmental impact (e.g., embodied carbon, recyclability, emissions).

Once you've made your selections, our system calculates an overall Eco-Score and estimated cost for your custom car. Then, you can simulate a race against a "standard" F1 car to see how your eco-innovations perform on the track!

The Vision: Engineering Meets Precision

In motorsport, tiny aerodynamic refinements can mean the difference between victory and defeat. Our tool lets users visualize airflows, adjust wing angles, and understand how small modifications affect real-world racing physics. Whether you're aiming for maximum stability or top speed, you’ll learn how to make an F1 car cut through the air like a champion.

How It Works

The F1 Aerodynamics Tester allows users to customize their car’s aerodynamic setup with adjustable parameters such as:

  • Front Wing & Rear Wing Angle: Controls the balance between grip and speed.
  • Drag Reduction System (DRS): Simulates the effect of DRS activation on acceleration.
  • Sidepod Intake Optimization: Adjusts airflow efficiency for cooling and aerodynamic stability.
  • Wind Tunnel Mode: Enlarges the testing area to analyze airflow patterns in greater detail.

Once users fine-tune their settings, they can visualize how air interacts with the car, observe real-time turbulence simulations, and compare their setup against a standard F1 configuration.

Optimization Tips & Ideal Ranges

Understanding how aerodynamics work is key to achieving the best balance of speed, grip, and efficiency. Here are a few guidelines to help you refine your setup:

  • Downforce: A well-balanced F1 setup usually maintains downforce between 2500–5000 N. Too much downforce increases grip but slows straight-line speed.
  • Drag: Ideally, keep drag below 3000 N to ensure efficient acceleration. Adjusting wing angles and using DRS can help reduce drag.
  • Wing Angle Adjustments: Lower front and rear wing angles will reduce drag, but at the cost of grip. Experiment with small changes for the best aerodynamic efficiency.
  • Sidepod Intake: Enhancing the sidepod intake can boost downforce slightly while reducing drag if optimized correctly.
  • Wind Tunnel Analysis: Use Wind Tunnel Mode to examine airflow disturbances and refine your setup accordingly.

Why This Matters

Aerodynamics is the invisible force dictating success on the track. By experimenting with different configurations, users can gain a deeper understanding of how teams optimize their cars for high-speed racing while minimizing drag losses.
