üìÖ UAE Racing Timeline

Scroll through the interactive timeline below to witness UAE‚Äôs motorsport evolution.

1981 - First Motor Racing Club

The Emirates Motor Sports Federation (EMSF) was established, laying foundational groundwork for organized motorsport in the UAE.

2004 - Dubai Autodrome Opens

A multi-purpose motorsport facility opens, becoming a central hub for racing activities, including touring cars and GT racing.

2009 - Yas Marina Circuit & F1 Debut

Abu Dhabi hosted its first Formula 1 race at the newly built Yas Marina Circuit, marking UAE‚Äôs grand entry into global motorsport.

2010 - Gulf 12 Hours Inauguration

The Gulf 12 Hours endurance race began at Yas Marina Circuit, establishing the UAE as a key player in international endurance racing.

2015 - UAE Karting Revolution

Young Emirati racers gained international recognition in global karting competitions, boosting grassroots motorsport and talent development.

2018 - Dubai Autodrome Solar Project

Installation of solar panels at Dubai Autodrome as part of its commitment to environmental sustainability and reducing carbon footprint.

2019 - Amna Al Qubaisi F1 Test

Amna Al Qubaisi becomes the first Emirati female to drive a Formula 1 car during a test session in Abu Dhabi, marking a historic moment.

2023 - F1 Academy Races at Yas Marina

Yas Marina Circuit hosts races for F1 Academy, a new all-female single-seater racing series, further promoting female participation in motorsport.

2023 - Sustainable Fuel Adoption (Local Races)

Local racing series begin trials and adoption of sustainable fuels, reducing environmental impact and aligning with global motorsport's green initiatives.
